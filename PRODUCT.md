# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HackVillage serves African technology-event participants and the people running or judging their work: developers entering events, organizers managing accountable prize-backed programs, and judges providing structured feedback. Authentication is their trust threshold: they need to understand where they are, enter quickly in local development, and never be confused about which workspace they may access.

## Product Purpose

HackVillage provides trustworthy infrastructure for high-impact technology events, connecting prize escrow, proof of work, and career opportunity. Authentication establishes a clear, enforceable role boundary before a user can access event operations, participant workspaces, or judging tools.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, exacting, optimistic. The product should feel like dependable civic infrastructure with the energy of a good local technology community, not a generic finance dashboard or an exclusive gatekeeper.

## Anti-references

Avoid neon-on-black developer aesthetics, anonymous corporate SSO walls, decorative glass cards, surveillance-like security language, and playful consumer onboarding that obscures permissions. Do not make role selection feel irreversible or intimidating.

## Design Principles

1. Make trust tangible: explain why access is scoped and show the active role plainly.
2. Remove uncertainty at the threshold: every sign-in, redirect, failure, and denied access state gives a useful next step.
3. Respect momentum: event participants should be able to complete access tasks without unnecessary friction.
4. Keep authority legible: permissions are enforced by the server and communicated in clear human language.
5. Build for the whole village: strong contrast, keyboard paths, reduced motion, and mobile-first actions are baseline requirements.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. All authentication actions must be keyboard-operable, expose focus state and errors to assistive technology, preserve readable contrast, and honor reduced-motion preferences. Copy should use clear, international English without relying on color alone.
